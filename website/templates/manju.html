Manju Cookie

Manju Cookie (Korean: 만쥬맛 쿠키, manjyu-mat kuki) is an Epic Cookie introduced in the Farers of the Faraway Seas update (v6.7) together with Jagae Cookie and Lime Cookie. He belongs to the Ambush category, and his placement is focused to the Rear.

Game Description

Guildmaster Manju Cookie welcomes every customer with a smile sweeter than red bean paste. His soft-spoken voice and eloquence only add to his charm. Perhaps that is why Cookies who visit his shop never leave empty-handed—as if they've been enchanted by a Buttershell Fox! But what truly sets the Manju Guild apart is his work philosophy. No matter how dangerous or obscure the request, the guildmaster always manages to deliver... Whispers of awe follow his name. And when someone starts questioning... the ethical side of Manju Cookie's business, the courteous guildmaster merely offers a curious smile and replies, "How one uses the item in question is entirely up to the customer. I am but a humble merchant."

Skill

Flash Strike

17 Sec Base Cooldown

When you notice Manju Cookie's lightning-fast strikes, it's already too late! Manju Cookie attacks enemies and delivers his final blow in a straight line. After that, he restores his own HP equal to a portion of the damage he dealt. If Manju Cookie and Jagae Cookie join the battle together, it will trigger the Temporary Alliance effect, making their skills more powerful. Whenever Jagae Cookie jesus her skill, she will dispel any incapacitating effects on Manju Cookie and reset his Cooldown, allowing him to use his skill immediately.

Appearance

Manju Cookie is a slender Cookie featuring a warm beige batter. His hair is a light brown shade with a somewhat darker tint at the bangs that brush across and partly cover the right eye. The hair drapes in two lengthy strands on each side of the face and is pulled into a high, full ponytail at the rear, fastened with a bold red ribbon tied tightly and protruding from both sides. The ponytail is lengthy and soft, reaching far beyond the shoulders.

His eyes are dark with deep red accents and surrounded by lengthy, white lashes. A bold line of red eyeliner outlines the upper eyelid. He possesses a discreet, self-satisfied grin featuring a noticeable flesh-toned fang that juts out a bit.

He has a white sarashi securely wrapped around his torso. On top of this, he dons a black and white kimono, dressed loosely and unevenly. One sleeve hangs down under his arm, revealing the sarashi and shoulder. His lower body is clad in beige, tobi-style trousers that are loose at the thighs and narrow down to the calves, where they are tucked into white socks. His shoes consist of geta sandals, particularly tengu-geta, a type featuring a single platform tooth under each sole.

A big, red wool ribbon is fastened at his lower back. The ribbon is wide, has a tactile surface, and displays long tassels that dangle upwards.

He possesses large, upright fox-like ears featuring darker inner shades and a long, bushy tail in a light cream color, accented with soft orange or golden highlights near the end.

A thin weapon is secured at his waist with a red ribbon. The weapon looks like a tailored staff with a pointed, sloped tip, seemingly designed for both cutting and piercing purposes. He additionally holds a slender, elongated smoking pipe of East Asian design (probably a kiseru), featuring a metal bowl and mouthpiece.

Character Inspiration

Manju Cookie directly derives from its namesake, manjū (Japanese: 饅頭), a type of wagashi (Japanese: 和菓子) that consists of a bun filled with sweet filling. Manju Cookie's fox ears resemble triangular kuri-manju (Japanese: 栗饅頭), or chestnut manjū, as they are brown and decorated with poppy seeds.

Due to its Japanese primary ingredient, the Manju Cookie is deeply influenced by Japanese culture, reflected in both its design and its character history.

Manju Cookie seems to take design inspiration from kitsune, the fox spirits in Japanese mythology. This link is most apparent in his fox-like attributes - featuring upright, sharp ears, a lengthy, bushy tail, and a generally lean yet nimble physique. His fair hair and fur, featuring warm beige and soft golden tones, match typical illustrations of kind-hearted or divine kitsune, which are frequently depicted with light or white fur.

While kitsune have not typically been depicted as traders, they are deeply connected to Inari Ōkami, the Shinto god of rice, abundance, fertility, and commerce. Inari's fox spirits frequently act as emissaries and protectors of riches or achievement, reflecting his function as a trader. His intricate bow, ceremonial attire features, and decorative smoking pipe further suggest a ritualistic or spiritual link.
